f-Socials Public Methodology

How we parse content to keep it a clean, unbiased evidence table:

1. Primary Sourcing Invariant: No factual claim is featured in our ledger unless backed by verifiable, indexed primary resources (court records, legal filings, published research). We will not guess or let the AI speculate.

2. Structural Labeling: Rather than diagnosing the author's intent ("bad faith" or "untruthful"), we focus exclusively on identifying patterns of framing (such as isolating specific sentences, truncated timelines, or loaded contrasts) so that readers can develop their own critical evaluation skills.

3. Non-Polarizing Bridging: Alternative sources are programmatically selected based on semantic alignment to provide highly diverse but constructive perspectives. We explicitly filter out dehumanizing arguments or poor-quality content, focusing instead on presenting varied analytical viewpoints (such as policy, legal, or technical lenses).
